Healthcare is often called retirement planning's blind spot. In Canada, provincial health insurance covers physician and hospital services — but the costs that hit retirees hardest are the ones it doesn't cover.

## What Provincial Health Insurance Covers

All provinces cover:
- Physician services (GP, specialists, surgery)
- Hospital stays
- Some home care (limited)
- Some long-term care (waitlisted, not guaranteed)

## What It Doesn't Cover (The Out-of-Pocket Reality)

**Dental care:** Routine cleanings, fillings, crowns, dentures, and implants are not covered provincially for most adults. The new federal dental benefit covers seniors over 70 with income below $90,000 starting in 2025 — but coverage is limited.

Average dental costs in retirement: $1,500–$4,000/year, rising with age.

**Prescription medications:** Provinces provide limited drug benefits for seniors (Ontario's ODB program covers some medications for 65+), but coverage gaps remain significant, especially for specialty drugs.

**Vision care:** Eye exams, glasses, and contact lenses are typically not covered after childhood in most provinces (Ontario covers annual exams for 65+).

**Hearing aids:** Not covered provincially. Average cost: $3,000–$7,000 per pair, replaced every 5–7 years.

**Physiotherapy, massage, chiropractic:** Limited or no coverage without private insurance.

**Home care and personal support:** Provincial programs exist but have long waitlists and limited hours. Private supplemental home care: $25–$40/hour.

## Long-Term Care: The Big Wildcard

This is the largest potential healthcare expense in retirement.

Ontario has two types of LTC:
- **Government-subsidized LTC homes:** Waitlisted. Basic private room: $2,701/month (2025). Semi-private: $2,418/month. Subsidized for lower-income residents.
- **Retirement homes (private):** Independent living to assisted living. $3,000–$8,000/month and more.

**Average LTC stay:** 2.5 years for those who enter a facility. Not everyone needs LTC — about 40% of Canadians die without ever requiring LTC facility placement. But for those who do, the cost is significant.

Average LTC cost at $3,500/month for 2.5 years: **$105,000**.

High-care retirement home at $6,000/month for 3 years: **$216,000**.

## Costs by Phase of Retirement

| Age | Primary Healthcare Costs | Estimated Annual |
|-----|------------------------|-----------------|
| 65–70 | Dental, medications, vision | $3,000–$6,000 |
| 70–80 | Above + hearing, home care | $5,000–$12,000 |
| 80–90 | Above + significant home care | $10,000–$25,000 |
| 90+ | LTC or intensive home care | $30,000–$90,000 |

These are broad estimates. Individual health status varies enormously.

## How to Budget for Healthcare in Retirement

**Option 1: Extended health insurance**
Private plans for retirees cover dental, drugs, vision, and some home care. Cost for a couple: $400–$900/month. This provides cost certainty but you pay premiums even in healthy years.

**Option 2: Self-insure with a healthcare reserve**
Keep $150,000–$300,000 in liquid, accessible savings (TFSA is ideal) earmarked for healthcare. In healthy years the money grows; in health events it's available.

**Option 3: Critical illness or long-term care insurance**
LTC insurance premiums are expensive ($200–$600/month for a 60-year-old) and the products have changed significantly. Critical illness insurance pays a lump sum on diagnosis of specified conditions. Both are worth investigating in your 50s before health conditions develop.

## The Ontario Drug Benefit for Seniors

Ontario's ODB program provides coverage for approved medications to residents 65+. There is a $100 annual deductible and a $6.11 dispensing fee per prescription. High-cost drugs may require prior authorization.

This provides meaningful coverage but not comprehensive coverage — especially for newer specialty medications.

## The Federal Dental Benefit (2025)

The Canadian Dental Care Plan covers routine dental care for seniors 65+ with net income below $90,000. Coverage is tiered based on income:
- Under $70,000: 80% of eligible costs covered
- $70,000–$80,000: 60% covered
- $80,000–$90,000: 40% covered

This is a meaningful benefit for many seniors that was not available until recently. Check eligibility at canada.ca/dental.
